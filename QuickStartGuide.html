Progressive learning

The primary goal of progressive learning is to keep prediction models up to date using the most recent data available as well as ensuring constant accuracy improvement over time. This service is a part of a larger HITL (Human-In-The-Loop) concept. This concept requires that auto coded records generated by the PROD Services should be reviewed by a human expert. The exact flow may vary (e.g., predictions with a low confidence score or claims identified as those that have a high risk of denial by the insurance company), but the idea is that some subset will be manually reviewed, and corrections will be introduced if required. In addition, when some unspecified or mismatched codes are found, they will be used to further the re-training of the models, so that accuracy of the coding improves. This process is known as progressive learning (alternatively on-line, continuous, active) and is one of the most reliable and robust machine learning techniques used to boost prediction accuracy in production systems.

CPT & HCPCS MODIFIERS

A modifier provides indicates that a service or procedure that has been performed has been altered by some specific circumstance but not changed in its definition or code.

For Medicare purposes, modifiers are two-digit codes that may consist of alpha and/or numeric characters, which may be appended to Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System (HCPCS) procedure codes to provide additional information needed to process a claim. This includes HCPCS Level 1, also known as Current Procedural Terminology® (CPT®) codes, and HCPCS Level II codes. Modifiers answer questions such as: which one, how many, what kind and when.

A vocabulary expansion for procedures was provided in a semi-automatic way and consisted of a combination of HCPCS \ CPT codes and modifiers. Before performing the automated activities, medical experts searched open source materials such as instructions for insurance companies, instructions for medical coders, etc. The purpose of this search was to determine the valid combinations of codes and CPT \ HCPCS modifiers. Automatic activities were performed using a script combining codes and modifiers. For this iteration of vocabulary expansion, a range of codes was chosen that was used for anesthesia. The use of modifiers with this range can have a large impact on insurance payouts.

ICD10 (International Classification of Diseases)

ICD is the foundation for the identification of health trends and statistics globally, and the international standard for reporting diseases and health conditions. It is the diagnostic classification standard for all clinical and research purposes. ICD defines the universe of diseases, disorders, injuries, and other related health conditions, listed in a comprehensive, hierarchical fashion that allows for:

  1. Easy storage, retrieval and analysis of health information for evidenced-based decision-making; and
  2. Sharing and comparing health information between hospitals, regions, settings and countries; and
  3. Data comparisons in the same location across different time periods.

The classification is divided into 21 chapters:

  • A00-B99 Certain infectious and parasitic diseases
  • C00-D49 Neoplasms
  • D50-D89 Diseases of the blood and blood-forming organs and certain disorders involving the immune mechanism
  • E00-E89 Endocrine, nutritional and metabolic diseases
  • F01-F99 Mental, Behavioral and Neurodevelopmental disorders
  • G00-G99 Diseases of the nervous system
  • H00-H59 Diseases of the eye and adnexa
  • H60-H95 Diseases of the ear and mastoid process
  • I00-I99 Diseases of the circulatory system
  • J00-J99 Diseases of the respiratory system
  • K00-K95 Diseases of the digestive system
  • L00-L99 Diseases of the skin and subcutaneous tissue
  • M00-M99 Diseases of the musculoskeletal system and connective tissue
  • N00-N99 Diseases of the genitourinary system
  • O00-O9A Pregnancy, childbirth and the puerperium
  • P00-P96 Certain conditions originating in the perinatal period
  • Q00-Q99 Congenital malformations, deformations and chromosomal abnormalities
  • R00-R99 Symptoms, signs and abnormal clinical and laboratory findings, not elsewhere classified
  • S00-T88 Injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes
  • V00-Y99 External causes of morbidity
  • Z00-Z99 Factors influencing health status and contact with health services
  • Codes U00–U49 are to be used by WHO for the provisional assignment of new diseases of uncertain etiology.
  • Codes U50–U99 may be used in research, e.g. when testing an alternative subclassification for a special project.

The chapters are subdivided into homogeneous ‘blocks’ of three-character categories. As example Chapter I, the block titles reflect two axes of classification – mode of transmission and broad group of infecting organisms. For Chapter II, the first axis is the behavior of the neoplasm; within behavior, the axis is mainly by site, although a few three-character categories are provided for important morphological types (e.g., leukemias, lymphomas, melanomas, mesotheliomas, Kaposi sarcoma).

Extension for CPT\HCPCS

Language library vocabulary includes the brand name of the drugs. This addition seeks to increase the accuracy of the coding of the auto coder when reading the name of a drug(s) in free text without specifying the ingredients.

Example:

Before adding Brand Names into free text “Nexplanon“ will result in “unspecified” as result. After adding free text “Nexplanon“ the code S0180 is rendered - “ETONOGESTREL (CONTRACEPTIVE) IMPLANT SYSTEM”

The addition of drugs was carried out in a semi-automatic way. RxNorm ontology was sourced, which contains the attributive relationship between the ingredient and the brand, then a connection was made between CPT\HCPCS codes. Human tasks Manual verified the correctness of the relationship between the ingredient, the brand, and the CPT\HCPCS code. Manual addition of some brands for codes was made.

                                        Evaluation steps

                                        1. End day retrieval

                                          end_day = data.get('end_day', 15), defaults to 15, has to be present for predictions to work.

                                            FacadeModel is a combined class, which instantiates 3 different models from all_models.py,

                                            calls ‘preprocess_and_transform_data’ method from preprocess_data.py, and returns the following predictions dictionary, containing results for each model:

                                            prediction_results = {'payment': payment_model.predict(data),

                                            'likelihoods': likelihood_model.predict(data, end_day=end_day),

                                            'similarity': similarity_model.predict(data)

                                            }

                                            All models, present if FacadeModel class:

                                            • SimilarityModel - Predict similarity score between pool of informative (i.e. ‘good’) claims and the given claim.
                                            • PaymentModelPerNpi - Predict payment amount for each claim based on different claim information. Model uses a model per each npi.
                                            • LikelihoodModel - Predict likelihood of payment per each procedure in the given claim and likelihoods over time

                                              Prediction List:

                                              1st item - prediction for the 1st day,

                                              2nd item - prediction for the 2nd day,

                                              3rd item - prediction for the 3rd day,

                                              … etc

                                        1. Results are calculated via FacadeModel’s predict method.

                                          In case of data validation error (border conditions) the respective error with a message is raised.

                                          1. Results as well as input data (claim_info) is jsonified and sent as response.

                                            jsonify(claim_info=claim_info, predictions=results)

                                        Testing

                                        Overview

                                        The testing approach included managing the complexity of multiple, independently deployable components in the Veuu ecosystem.

                                        The testing strategy employed aimed to provide coverage to each layer of the ecosystem as well as the in between layers of the service (e.g., Each API connection). The testing is was designed to be specific and remain lightweight in design.

                                        The connections out to external services required special attention since they cross network boundaries. Regardless, the system must be resilient to outages of remote components. With built in alert logic to the various gateways, we were also able to test remote error cases (e.g., issues that may be unlikely to take place, yet the system and the maintenance teams need to properly prepare).

                                        Within the Veuu ecosystem, microservices handle requests by passing messages between each of the relevant modules to form a response. A particular request may require interaction with services and gateways so the connections between modules are loosely defined. The automated tests on the Veuu ecosystem provided coverage for each of these communications at a granular level. As a result, each test provided a focused and fast feedback cycle.

                                        Typically, communications with external services are more coarsely grained than the equivalent in process communications to prevent API chattiness and latency. It should be noted that, the testing concerns for external services (e.g., Epic, Cerner, Signature Bank) can be different to those for services under the team control since less guarantees can be made about the interface and availability of an external team's services (99.9999% up time guarantee only includes those services under the maintenance team’s control).

                                        ICD: 1,000 ICD records were chosen at random from out of sample data that had been assigned a denied adjudication reason code on multiple tries. These diagnoses were deemed to be the most difficult diagnosis to code based on the original denials.

                                        CPT: 2,000 CPT records were chosen at random from out of sample data. Some of these records had been assigned a denied adjudication code whereas some had been accepted and adjudicated on the first submission. Procedures had a range of two words to twenty sixteen words.

                                        Risk Modeling: 1,000 records were chosen at random from out of sample data. It was a requirement that each record required the proper fields for the risk modeling system to be applied. The records chosen all had the necessary fields of data required for the risk modeling system to make a payment prediction. In the cases where the risk modeling received ICD or CPT records to assign confidence to coding performed by the coder, those records also had the required fields necessary for the risk modeling system to run its models on an NPI (by hospital) model.

                                        Since many of the integrated components in the Veuu ecosystem have never worked together before, component testing was one of the most important tests to be conducted. A component test limited the scope of the exercised software to a portion of the system under test, manipulating the system through internal code interfaces and using test doubles to isolate the code under test from other components.

                                        A component is defined as, any well-encapsulated, coherent, and independently replaceable part of a larger system. The Veuu components are:

                                        • AI Coder
                                        • AI Risk Modeler
                                        • Progressive Learning
                                        • Epic Connector
                                        • Cerner Connector
                                        • Hyperledger blockchain
                                        • Payments platform

                                        Isolating the component from its peers avoids any complex behavior they may have. It also helped to provide a controlled testing environment for the component, triggering any applicable error cases in a repeatable manner.

                                        By writing tests at this granularity, the contract of the ecosystem API to the component was driven through tests from the perspective of a customer. Isolation of the service was achieved by replacing external collaborators with mocks and stubs and by using internal API endpoints to probe or configure the service. They were used to emulate unhappy paths through the component such as when external collaborators were offline or are responding slowly or with malformed responses. This allowed error conditions to be tested in a controlled and repeatable manner. In this vein, each API was tested independently for failure. The AI powered coder was isolated from the API and was tasked with processing claim records for coding as noted earlier in the report. The AI powered risk system was isolated from the API and was tasked with processing coded claims and payments on an independent basis as noted earlier in the report.
